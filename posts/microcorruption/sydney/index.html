Microcorruption

Sydney

Here we have four ‘cmp’ instructions on two bytes of r15, respectively incrementing the offset by two each time. The first ‘cmp’ checks, whether the first two bytes are 0x466a, the next two #0x6158 and so on. 
448a <check_password>
448a:  bf90 6a46 0000 cmp	#0x466a, 0x0(r15)
4490:  0d20           jnz	$+0x1c
4492:  bf90 5861 0200 cmp	#0x6158, 0x2(r15)
4498:  0920           jnz	$+0x14
449a:  bf90 6741 0400 cmp	#0x4167, 0x4(r15)
44a0:  0520           jne	#0x44ac <check_password+0x22>
44a2:  1e43           mov	#0x1, r14
44a4:  bf90 6363 0600 cmp	#0x6363, 0x6(r15)
44aa:  0124           jeq	#0x44ae <check_password+0x24>
44ac:  0e43           clr	r14
44ae:  0f4e           mov	r14, r15
44b0:  3041           ret

We can either look for the corresponding ascii representation or write the hex representation in swapped order, as we have a little endian format here: 

>>> "6a46586167416363".decode('hex')
